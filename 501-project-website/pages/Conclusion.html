CONCLUSION

It is true that we take climate change and the environment for granted more than we should. The purpose was to analyze climate change trends and how humans are mostly responsible for these trends. Based on the data I gathered, I concluded that there has been a considerable change in temperature, which has been rapidly increasing.
The Original data shows that highest variation in average temperature was recorded in India in past six years. China is right behind India and Canada has recorded third highest increse in temperature.

Naive Bayes on Temperature data.

dataset was trained to predict temperature forecasts in several nations based on historical temperature data, and the model is 95% accurate in most cases, predicting higher temperatures than projected temperatures. This indicates that the temperature is rising and that we are on the verge of global warming. The data for the temperature record for the previous five years is collected, the expectation assumptions are formed, and the Bayes model is used to forecast the temperature difference. The accuracy of the models was 95% in each case. The precision for this data is fairly good, indicating that the model can accurately forecast temperature variance. More data can be used to improve the model.

The link between humans impacting the environment and the environment affecting humans is obvious, but for the time being, I’d like to focus on the human impact on climate change. The large temperature shift is caused by the release of hazardous components into the atmosphere, such as carbon dioxide and methane.

As an illustration of how humans emit dangerous components into the atmosphere, I gathered data on emissions caused by waste food disposal and crop residue in the United States. I chose these two domains because agricultural cultivation and food disposal are fundamental human activities. If we can inflict environmental damage through everyday activities, the repercussions of a nuclear power plant are unthinkable.

Naive Bayes on Food Disposal Crop Residue Data

The model has been trained to recognize the emission element. The model was trained to classify various emission constituents. The data set was divided into two parts: training and testing. The model correctly classified the items 74% of the time. The model forecasts the emission based on the domain and item that triggered it. Food disposal and crop residue are the domains considered here. The prediction aids in avoiding hazardous emissions such as CO2 caused by any crop or food disposal process, and may be able to identify an alternative for that process.

SVM on Food Disposal and Crop Residue Data

The same data set with the same purpose was used to classify the emissions using SVM, various kernels were used to see which kernel gave the highest accuracy. Linear kernel was the most accurate with 89% accuracy

Air Quality Data: The data set gives an overview of toxins present in the air in New York city and how it’s effects on people. I chose this data set to analyse the consequences of climate change on humans.

Naive Bayes on Air Quality data.

The naive bayes model is applied to find out the conditional probability of air quality, people affected by the pollution and their age group. To do this, data on air quality and its effects on individuals was analyzed. After separating the model into train and test data, the model was trained to predict air quality, age group, and number of persons impacted by pollution. The model has an accuracy of 84%.

SVM on Air Quality data:

The degree of precision generated by different kernels varied. Finally, the linear kernel is ideally suited for the most accurate data. With a 70% accuracy, the model forecasts the age group of people affected by air pollution in New York City.

Naive Bayes is the best fit for this data. Because the data set is quite huge, Naive Bayes is the best prediction method.

Clustering on Air Quality data:

This data is clustered to get insight into the underlying patterns of several concentration measures. The purpose of clustering was to look for similarities in the toxicity of air quality. To get the outcome, three distinct clustering techniques were used, K-means, density-based spatial clustering with noise, and hierarchical clustering. The model demonstrates that the toxicities aren’t very diverse to one other in an attempt to discover similarities within the air toxicity.

Naive Bayes on Text data

I applied Naive Bayes on twitter data collected on two hash tags, climatechange and savetheplanet to understand the similiraties and dissimilarities between these two hashtags.
